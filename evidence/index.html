Supporting research and documentation.

These sources support the scientific rationale, frugal engineering direction, and team credibility behind ForAll Sense. They do not constitute regulatory clearance or validated performance claims for a finished commercial device.

Primary research

Raman Barcode for Counterfeit Drug Product Detection

This FDA-authored Analytical Chemistry paper supports the core idea that Raman methods can screen finished drug products, not just pure APIs, by comparing meaningful spectral peaks rather than relying on a one-to-one library match.

Claim supported

Raman workflows can rapidly distinguish authentic and counterfeit product signatures in a way that is relevant to counterfeit screening.

Why it matters

It underpins the top-of-page story about light-based screening without overstating product readiness.

DOI / article

Public-domain FDA study

Evaluation of Suspected Counterfeit Pharmaceutical Tablets Declared to Contain Controlled Substances Using Handheld Raman Spectrometers

This U.S. FDA study describes a rapid, field-friendly counterfeit tablet workflow using handheld Raman devices, including parent-to-daughter device transfer and nonexpert usage. It is especially useful for explaining why the site emphasizes triage and prioritization rather than final confirmatory testing.

Claim supported

Handheld Raman screening has been studied in practical field workflows for counterfeit tablets and can support rapid triage.

Why it matters

It gives the homepage a stronger field-operations frame and provides a public-domain government source.

Low-cost instrumentation

Detection Method of Falsified Medicines by Using a Low-Cost Raman Scattering Spectrometer Combined with SIMCA and PLS-DA

This study supports the lower-cost instrumentation angle by explicitly evaluating a low-cost Raman analyzer for falsified medicines distributed in the market. It is one of the clearest public references for the idea that compact and lower-cost Raman screening can be practical.

Claim supported

Low-cost Raman analyzers, paired with suitable chemometric methods, can support falsified-medicine detection.

Why it matters

It helps us talk about affordability and portability without implying this website already represents a market-ready commercial instrument.
